Tastes of Saudi Arabia

Bukhori and Shawarma are two traditional delights that hold a special place in the rich culinary tapestry of Saudi Arabia. Bukhori, a fragrant rice dish infused with aromatic spices, is a testament to the kingdom's love for flavorful and hearty meals. This dish often features tender meat, such as lamb or chicken, adding a savory dimension to the perfectly seasoned rice. On the other hand, Shawarma, a popular street food, showcases the influence of Middle Eastern cuisine in Saudi Arabia. Thinly sliced marinated meat, typically chicken or beef, is slow-cooked on a vertical rotisserie, resulting in succulent layers that are then wrapped in flatbread. The combination of spices and the unique cooking method imparts a distinct taste to Shawarma, making it a beloved and easily recognizable dish across the country. Together, Bukhori and Shawarma exemplify the diverse and delicious offerings found in Saudi Arabian culinary traditions. For those planning to have a trip to Saudi, I recommend getting the taste of Shawarma before leaving as it is currently my favourite street food from Saudi Arabia.
